RBs in 2022 Part 2

TL;DR

  • Quality backs can be effective in different ways. Backs that put up god awful rushing numbers can still be in the RB1 and RB2 tier.
  • ETN flashed signs last year - for those that paid attention and pounced during the offseason, congrats on getting him! For those that didn't, he's still worth paying up for.
  • Swift is worth buying, even if his price has gone up. He has a soft playoff schedule and I think he will only get better given his efficiency and how good the situation is in Philly.
  • Monty is similar to Swift from a value perspective, but I would pay less for him. He isn't as talented as Swift and Monty is a little older. He's the Jamaal Williams Deluxe of this season.
  • Conner still has some juice in him - very much worth the risk, especially with an IR discount.
  • Pierce feels like a decent buy low with the expectation of getting some decent numbers for this year, if only on volume. I don't think he is a future proof back by any means.
  • Raschaad White is putrid. Sell where you can, if you can. The outlook on White for me is a worse Pierce... but wait until he strings together a few PPR performances.
  • Taylor is a good future investment, his down year still led to great numbers. Now that his contract is sorted and AR looks the part, he has a high ceiling for upcoming seasons.

Overview

This week I go ahead and continue my analysis on running back performance in 2022, by using my clustering tooling to examine tiers of running backs.

I would like to point out that I've updated my clustering to be interactive as well, so you can hover over players in the graph to see their numbers and figure out what cluster they are in.

For those that want to go ahead and check out part one of my running back analysis, check out this page.

Other analysis pieces can be found here.

My data disclaimer, as always:

  • I don't take into account anything related to two point conversions, partly due to the randomness and small sample size but mostly because it was difficult to incorporate into the data I fetched
  • The top 40 RBs I analyze are by the top 40 fantasy point totals, this is done for simplicity and reusability of graphing code

After taking a gander at the graph, I would classify the clusters as the following: Cluster 0 as "The Anemic Rushers", Cluster 1 is more of the "Eh, it's a day job" type, and Cluster 2 gives off "Hulk SMASH!" vibes.

These observations pretty much throwout fumbles and yards per carry, as each of the clusters are all over the place for those stats. With that being taken into account, Cluster 0 is towards the bottom in rushing yards, carries, and rushing touchdowns, while Cluster 2 is killing it in that regard, and Cluster 1 fits snuggly inbetween.

I'm surprised that both Leonard Fournette and Jerick McKinnon were able to put up the seasons (RB12 and RB20, respectively in PPR) they did while being so bad at putting such bad rushing related stats.

I guess you can find yourself a viable fantasy back that doesn't have a large impact in the running game. Something to keep an eye out for during any season, as a solid contributor could be had on the cheap.

On that note, Raschaad White concerns me here, especially since the per game numbers (we will get there) don't reflect well for him either. I'm a little surprised he's kept his job this year, I am very high on Sean Tucker, but I might have been wrong on my Tucker take. I'd probably try and move of White is possible, he is a back to me that is very replaceable, from an NFL roster perspective. since the

Moving onto Cluster 1, I am surprised a few backs are in this group instead of the rushers that grab the most stats.

CMC and ETN are the two that were the most shocking for me, as they had excellent rushing yard and carry stats. CMC had 1139 rushing yards on 244 carries and ETN had 1125 rushing yards on 220 carries. Their rushing touchdown numbers, 8 for CMC and 5 for ETN, keep them out of the elite rushing stat padders.

If you were also surprised by Mixon, Jones, Pollard, and Rhamondre, I was too at first, but their rushing and carry stats were definitely lower than both CMC and ETN. Touchdowns were spread out amongst this group. I will say this makes sense with what we are seeing from ETN this year, and even with that being said he might still be a good buy with his youth and the talent on that Jags offense.

Finally, our last tier to analyze: the monsters! These backs put up great rushing numbers last year, but some players in this list surprised me.

Ekeler was shocking to me, as I didn't think of him as a dominant rusher, and when checking the stats that seems to be true: 915 rushing yards and 204 carries... but 13 rushing touchdowns catapults him into this tier.

Outside of that, the name that stunned me here was Elliot.

Yes, Najee and Jamaal aren't sexy backs, but they get enough volume and touchdowns to sneak into that tier. Elliot gets himself into this category, despite putting up less than 900 rushing yards. His 12 rushing touchdowns and 231 carries seem to just barely get him into this tier.

Things shake up quite a bit when we get to six tiers.

We have our touchdown hogs - with a bit of a fumbling problem - in Cluster 1. All of these guys have at least 13 touchdowns on the ground and at least 3 fumbles.

The best tier of backs here, for these rushing stats, is Cluster 3. They all have at least 1139 rushing yards and 244 carries. Additionally, they are all decently efficient, with the yards per carry varying from 4.45 to 5.05. Their nose for the endzone isn't bad as the group varies from 8-12 touchdowns, and all of them have less than 3 fumbles. Word of caution - I would avoid Sanders in Carolina, as I think these numbers were largely a product of that Eagles oline and offense.

Cluster 5 remains unchanged, putrid rushers unite!

Cluster 0 and Cluster 2 both contain more middling backs. Not great at anything, but also not bad at anything. Cluster 2 is slightly better from an efficiency perspective and slightly worse with fumbles. Cluster 0 is better at getting into the endzone.

Cluster 4 contians our hyper-efficient backs, who don't really fumble. These backs don't get a ton of carries and have pretty meh rushing yardage totals. Their touchdowns are also on the middle to lower side of things, but holy hell are they extremely efficient. The least efficient back in this group, is Patterson with 4.83 yards per carry... the most efficient is Swift with 5.47 yards per carry!

I said it in part one, Swift is worth grabbing, especially as a contender. He's in a better situation than last year. The Eagles are handling him better and he's getting more opportunities. I think he could explode later in the season, especially in the playoffs against the Giants (week 16 at home) and the Cardinals (week 17 at home). I'd happily pay a first for him as a contender, and I would be ecstatic if he is my RB2 or RB3.
