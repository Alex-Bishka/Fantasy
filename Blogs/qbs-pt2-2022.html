Overview

Welcome back to yet another analysis piece on position group performance in 2022. This is part two for quarterbacks, where we be using passing stats to create quarterback tiers.

The goal is to find hidden value from these tiers, in other words: opportunities to add underappreciated talent to your roster.

Part one focuses on trends amongst quarterback stats to find points of arbitrage, so if you're interested in looking even further into the subject, I would recommned looking back at it.

For those curious about analysis on the other position groups in 2022, please check out the blogs page.

My data disclaimer, as always:

  • I don't take into account anything related to two point conversions, partly due to the randomness and small sample size but mostly because it was difficult to incorporate into the data I fetched
  • The top 30 QBs I analyze are by the top 40 fantasy point totals, this is done for simplicity and reusability of graphing code

Tiers (Season Totals)

The passing stats that we will evaluate quarterbacks on: "Passing Yards", "Attempts", "Completions", "Passing TDs", and "Interceptions".

A quick breakdown of each tier:

  • Cluster 0: Middling numbers, very much between Cluster 1 and Cluster 2. The exception is interceptions: this group is slightly worse than Cluster 1.
  • Cluster 1: QB 1 territory. These guys thorw the ball frequently, and accumulate a substantial amount of yards and touchdowns. Their interception numbers are about on par with Cluster 0.
  • Cluster 2: Quarterbacks who don't attempt or complete many passes. As a result, they don't have many passing yards, touchdowns or interceptions.

You definitely want a quarterback in Cluster 1. That is a sustainable and successful fantasy archetype for the quarterback position. Obviously not everyone will be Mahomes, but 2022 Goff, TLaw, Cousins, etc. offer very solid QB1 numbers.

Furthermore, you can get a decent start without breaking the bank like Brady. For this season, my favorite quarterback from this cluster - in terms of bang for your buck - was Goff.

I would still say Goff is at a good price: listed as QB14 on KTC (as of 10/3/23). That feels like a pretty sweet price for QB9 on the season. I am quite confident he will finish as a QB1 in that Detroit offense, and see no reason why he can't be a lower end QB1 for the foreseeable future in that situation.

Another observation worth mentioning: Cluster 2 has some star names in it. Looking at the per game numbers, Murray does improve, so I'll hold off my judgement on him for now.

Lamar, Fields, and Danny Dimes are all in Cluster 2. That concerns me. I love the running threat that each of them have, but I would want them to at least be in Cluster 0...

I said in in part one, but for the major deficiencies in the passing game, I would want out on Fields. Fields is such an outlier, it terrifies me. Could he turn it around? Maybe... but I am doubtful for many reasons.

Fields had two monster games in 2023, but they were against very favorable matchups: Denver and Washington. Every other game, he has looked meh at best. I would look to sell after a couple of monster rushing games.

I am a little more lenient on Jones and Jackson, because they both have showed much more in the passing game than Fields, but Jones would make me very uneasy. The Giants have such a bad oline and the Waller experiment hasn't really worked out.

The reason I'm more iffy on moving off of Jones is because I don't think you can recoup much value for him, and he still might offer a QB1/QB2 game here and there, which is useful in superflex. Fields, on the other hand, could still yield a good-to-great haul.

I am most comfortable riding out Jackson. He's having a really good season so far, and could beat his MVP season passing numbers, which are in the ball park of being in Cluster 2 above. That being said, I would consider a TLaw+ offer for Jackson, just for sustainablity concerns, running quarterbacks getting hurt a little too much for my taste.

A breakdown of each cluster:

  • Cluster 0: See Cluster 1 in the previous graph: "QB 1 territory. These guys thorw the ball frequently, and accumulate a substantial amount of yards and touchdowns. This group tends to throw more interceptions than clusters 1 and 2."
  • Cluster 1: Quarterbacks that do not throw the ball much - this depresses the rest of their passing stats.
  • Cluster 2: These qbs are in the middle across all passing stats - not super excited from any of their stats, but also not very disappointed.
  • Cluster 3: About on par with Cluster 2, but this group throws more interceptions.

Our QB1 tier, Cluster 0 in this case, remains pretty unchanged. The only change, here, when going from three tiers to four tiers is that Rodgers joins the group.

I don't think it would shock many people to see Rodgers be part of the better passing group(s) in this analysis. I was actually pretty high on him going into this season. He finished as QB13 last year with some minor injuries bothering him, and I believe the Jets offense puts him in a better position.

It's a shame that he tore his achilles on the first play of the year, but he might be worth a punt for next year. I wouldn't pay more than a 3rd, because it would probably be about one year of production at most.

The other noticeable change worth talking about with four tiers is that Hurts and Tua fall to a lower tier passing cluster.

I think this is an argument for being a bit more pessimistic on Hurts, compared to Allen and Mahomes. Hurts is great, but the lack of a strong passing base might prove to be the reason why he can't consistently shake up those two as the quarterbacks to have for the next five-to-ten years. It's the only knock against him joining or surpassing Allen and Mahomes.

I would like to withhold judgement on Tua until we hit the per game numbers, as he only played in 13 games last year, and in one of them he played only 39% of the snaps.

One more thing to add: Jones is in Cluster 2. To me this shows that his passing base is borderline legitamate, which is why I have less concerns for him long term, compared to, say, Fields.

I will say that Fields' ceiling is insanely high, but, throughout this analysis, I have developed serious questions about his sustainablity and future as a quarterback.

Can he stay healthy with all the running he does? Can he become a more consistent and better passer? If not, can he reliably deliver RB1 numbers? Is that a recipe for winning in the NFL? Will he still be a starting quarterback in a year or two with how things have gone for the Bears?

Too many concerns there for me to consider him a dynasty cornerstone piece.

A quick breakdown of the five clusters:

  • Cluster 0: Quarterbacks that do not attempt or complete many passes. Low passing stats across the board.
  • Cluster 1: The higher end of the QB1 scale, in regards to passing. High passing yards, attempts, completions, touchdowns, and interceptions.
  • Cluster 2: Middle of the pack quarterbacks that throw the most picks compared to other tiers.
  • Cluster 3: About on par with Cluster 2, but far less interceptions thrown.
  • Cluster 4: In contention with Cluster 1 for being the best tier here. The lower amount of passing touchdowns holds them back, but they do throw less interceptions.

Daniel Jones sticking with Hurts is interesting... perhaps he is Hurts lite? This season has been really tough for Jones, but I do wonder what would happen if the Giants gave him a good oline and a legitmate receiving threat or two. Might be a good buy low, as you shouldn't have to break the bank for him and the Giants seem committed to Jones.

Clusters 1 and 4 really catch my eye. Burrow sticking around with Mahomes and Allen seems reasonable, but Geno and Kirk too?

Geno revitalized himself last year, and I thought he would be a decent option in startups or re-draft, but he has struggled to capture the magic from 2022. I do expect him to improve in the second half of 2023 as he has some easier matchups, but probably someone I would stay away from unless the price was very cheap. For what it's worth Geno is currently QB21 on KTC (10/4/23), and is worth just above a 2025 early 2nd. However, for that price I would rather take a shot on Jordon Love or Danny Dimes.

Captain Kirk is a really solid fantasy quarterback, and him being in Cluster 1 confirms to me that his passing base is really, really good. After his achilles injury, he might be a great buy low for next year. It should be noted that he may no longer be a Viking, and who knows how this injury will affect him in the few remaining years of his career. However, he could also get a massive upgrade and end up somewhere like Atlanta. Kirk with those weapons would be pretty mouthwatering.

Now, Cluster 4 has three names that I would consider investing in as any dynasty franchise: TLaw, Goff, and Herbert. Each of these three should continue to deliver in this range, and I believe TLaw and Herbert could break into that Burrow range at some point (better than everyone else, but just below Mahomes and Allen).

For the record, I have paid up for TLaw and Herbert, but I see them as cornerstone pieces that are likely part of my rooster until they retire, only parting with them if I were to receive an insane offer for either.
