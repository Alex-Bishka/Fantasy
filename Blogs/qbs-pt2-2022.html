Overview

Welcome back to yet another analysis piece on position group performance in 2022. This is part two for quarterbacks, where we be using passing stats to create quarterback tiers.

The goal is to find hidden value from these tiers, in other words: opportunities to add underappreciated talent to your roster.

Part one focuses on trends amongst quarterback stats to find points of arbitrage, so if you're interested in looking even further into the subject, I would recommned looking back at it.

For those curious about analysis on the other position groups in 2022, please check out the blogs page.

My data disclaimer, as always:

  • I don't take into account anything related to two point conversions, partly due to the randomness and small sample size but mostly because it was difficult to incorporate into the data I fetched
  • The top 30 QBs I analyze are by the top 40 fantasy point totals, this is done for simplicity and reusability of graphing code

Tiers (Season Totals)

The passing stats that we will evaluate quarterbacks on: "Passing Yards", "Attempts", "Completions", "Passing TDs", and "Interceptions".

A quick breakdown of each tier:

  • Cluster 0: Middling numbers, very much between Cluster 1 and Cluster 2. The exception is interceptions: this group is slightly worse than Cluster 1.
  • Cluster 1: QB 1 territory. These guys thorw the ball frequently, and accumulate a substantial amount of yards and touchdowns. Their interception numbers are about on par with Cluster 0.
  • Cluster 2: Quarterbacks who don't attempt or complete many passes. As a result, they don't have many passing yards, touchdowns or interceptions.

You definitely want a quarterback in Cluster 1. That is a sustainable and successful fantasy archetype for the quarterback position. Obviously not everyone will be Mahomes, but 2022 Goff, TLaw, Cousins, etc. offer very solid QB1 numbers.

Furthermore, you can get a decent start without breaking the bank like Brady. For this season, my favorite quarterback from this cluster - in terms of bang for your buck - was Goff.

I would still say Goff is at a good price: listed as QB14 on KTC (as of 10/3/23). That feels like a pretty sweet price for QB9 on the season. I am quite confident he will finish as a QB1 in that Detroit offense, and see no reason why he can't be a lower end QB1 for the foreseeable future in that situation.

Another observation worth mentioning: Cluster 2 has some star names in it. Looking at the per game numbers, Murray does improve, so I'll hold off my judgement on him for now.

Lamar, Fields, and Danny Dimes are all in Cluster 2. That concerns me. I love the running threat that each of them have, but I would want them to at least be in Cluster 0...

I said in in part one, but for the major deficiencies in the passing game, I would want out on Fields. Fields is such an outlier, it terrifies me. Could he turn it around? Maybe... but I am doubtful for many reasons.

Fields had two monster games in 2023, but they were against very favorable matchups: Denver and Washington. Every other game, he has looked meh at best. I would look to sell after a couple of monster rushing games.

I am a little more lenient on Jones and Jackson, because they both have showed much more in the passing game than Fields, but Jones would make me very uneasy. The Giants have such a bad oline and the Waller experiment hasn't really worked out.

The reason I'm more iffy on moving off of Jones is because I don't think you can recoup much value for him, and he still might offer a QB1/QB2 game here and there, which is useful in superflex. Fields, on the other hand, could still yield a good-to-great haul.

I am most comfortable riding out Jackson. He's having a really good season so far, and could beat his MVP season passing numbers, which are in the ball park of being in Cluster 2 above. That being said, I would consider a TLaw+ offer for Jackson, just for sustainablity concerns, running quarterbacks getting hurt a little too much for my taste.
